# happygrass-happyforb
An experimental test of trait-based methods for ecological restoration.

These data and code were used to produce the analysis and figures in the associated manuscript where we tested a traiy-based framework for designing seed mixes for ecological restoraion in two distinct grassland types--annual California grassland and mixed-grass prarie in Wyoming. Based on analyses of compositional dissimilarities, we found trait-based restoration targets were more difficult to maintain in an annual-dominant grassland rather than perennial-dominant, and targets composed of multiple community-weighted mean traits were more difficult to meet than targets that maximized functional diversity. In both grasslands, plant communities with drought tolerant traits maintained growth rates under reduced precipitation, but these effects diminished after multiple years of drought and during drought release and were most effective at reistsing invasion. 
The "raw_cover" folder contains raw cover data for each species in each plot at each site (Species_Composition_allyears.csv in California and hpg_total.csv in Wyoming). The "soil_moisture" folder contains raw continuous soil moisture by block at each site (soil_moisture_ca/three_year_soil_moisture.csv in California and soil_moisture_wy contains three dataframes Restoration_soilmoisture_2021.csv, Restoration_soilmoisture_2022.csv, and Restoration_soilmoisture_2023(toAug).csv for soil moisture in Wyoming, as well as data on soil temperature soil_temp_master.csv for Wyoming). The "trait_data" folder contains species-level average trait data for all species at each site (annualgrass.csv in California and mixedgrass.csv in Wyoming (detailed information on these datasets are included in Appendix S2 of the accompanying publication). All other data (communty weighted mean traits, functional diversity, Bray-Curtis dissimilarity, Euclidean trait ditance to each seeding treatment target).
